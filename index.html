Hi there! I’m a senior at BITS Pilani - Goa, India, where I’m working towards an M.Sc. in Physics and a B.E. in Computer Science. I am currently working on my M.Sc. thesis, advised by Dr. Sergei Gleyzer, focused on developing diffusion-based generative models for detector reconstruction, with an emphasis on the CMS calorimeter module. Over the past year, I have also extensively worked with Dr. Daniel Murnane and Dr. Savannah Thais on developing symmetry-equivariant geometric deep learning models for tracking charged particles produced in proton-proton collisions at the LHC.

I grew up in Bangalore, India and in 2018, I moved to Goa to study Physics and Computer Science at BITS, Pilani. Here, I tried to strike a fine balance between both my majors by blending them at every chance I got. With Dr. Kinjal Banerjee and Dr. Raghunath Ratabole, I worked on a broad range of high-energy physics and field-theoretic topics ranging from nuclear equations of state to formulating scalar field theories in a wavelet basis, and I discovered the joys of deep learning working with Dr. Tirtharaj Dash on a range of generative modelling tasks.

My name is pronounced Ameya (“uh-may-yuh”) Thete (“thay-te”, with a hard t on the second syllable).
